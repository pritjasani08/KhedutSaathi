Glossary :: F
Fallow
Cropland left idle during the
growing season.
Forage
Herbaceous plants or plant parts
fed to domestic animals.
Forestry
The
science, art, and practice of managing and protecting tree and forest resources
for human benefit.
Fallow
land
Land
left unframed for one or more growing seasons to kill weeds make the soil
richer.
Farming
system
Farming
system is an integrated approach to farming with several enterprises (e.g.,
rice cultivation, pisciculture, horticulture, poultry and cattle dairying),
designed to be complementary to each other. A combination of one or more
enterprises with cropping, when carefully chosen, planned and executed, gives
greater dividends than a single enterprise, especially for small and marginal
farmers.
Field
irrigation efficiency
It is the ratio
expressed in the percentage of irrigation water available for use of crops to
that delivered to a field.
Filler
Any materials mixed with
fertilizers for any purpose other than the addition of available nutrients,
such as for conditioning to give anti-caking properties and for increasing the
weight to bring the percentage of nutrients to desired values.
Fodder
crops
The
cultivated plant species that are utilized as livestock feed in the form of
silage and hay.
Foundation
seed
The second look in the certified seed
chain produced from breeder seed and handled in such a way as to ensure genetic
identity and varietal purity.
