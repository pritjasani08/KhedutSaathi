Plant Nutrition :: Plant Growth Regulators
Introduction
Plant growth regulators may be defined as any
organic compounds, which are active at low concentrations (1-10 ng / nl) in
promoting, inhibiting or modifying growth and development. The naturally
occurring (endogenous) growth substances are commonly known as plant hormones,
while the synthetic ones are called growth regulator.
Plant
hormone is an organic compound synthesised in one part of the plant and
translocated to another part, where in very low concentrations it causes a physiological
response. The plant hormones are identified as promoters (auxins, gibberellin
and cytokinin), inhibitors (abscissic acid and ethylene) and other hypothetical
growth substance (florigen, flowering hormone,
etc
.,)

4. Etylene
