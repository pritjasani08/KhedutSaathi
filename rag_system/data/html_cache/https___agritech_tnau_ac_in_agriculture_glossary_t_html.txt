Glossary :: T
Tapping
The process of
harvesting latex from trunk of rubber plant, fruit of papaya, etc.
Thinning
:
Removing
of extra plants from thickly populated crop stand with an idea of maintaining
optimum plant population.
Tillage
The operation of
agriculture implements through the soil for the preparation of seed bed. It helps
loosen soil, incorporate fertilizers and leads to fine tilth aggregates.
Inadequate crop stand is a major reason for low crop yields in rainfed areas.
This is mainly because of the restricted entry of water into the soil due to
improper tillage practices. Tillage in rainfed areas agriculture therefore aims
at – Preparing a suitable seed bed; Controlling weeds; Conserving moisture;
Establishing uniform initial stand; Increasing water intake; Covering large
areas during the restricted sowing period using lesser amount of energy; and
maintaining the soil surface conditions. Land treatment works out to be
cost-effective in the long run because proper tillage is a low cost investment.
All one needs is a suitable implement and labour. There are different types of
tillage practices for different soil types. They include deep tillage,
off-season tillage and shallow tillage.
Topping (tobacco)
The removal of the
terminal bud with or without some of the small top leaves just before or after
the emergence of a flower bud.
Total mixed ration
A diet where all the feed ingredients are blended together
to ensure every bit is nutritionally balanced.
Transpiration
Transpiration is the
process by which water vapour is released into the atmosphere through the stomal
opening found on leaves and stem of plants.
