Farm Television programs
Indian small screen
programming started off  in the early
1980s. There were initially two ignition points: the first in the eighties when
colour TV was introduced by state-owned broadcaster Doordarshan (DD) timed with
the 1982 Asian Games which India hosted. It then proceeded to install
transmitters nationwide rapidly for terrestrial broadcasting. At that time
there was only one national channel
Doordarshan
.
The central government launched a series of economic and social reforms in 1991
under Prime Minister Narasimha Rao. Under the new policies the government
allowed private and foreign broadcasters to engage in limited operations in
India.
The second spark came
in the early nineties with the broadcast of satellite TV by foreign programmers
like CNN followed by Star TV and a little later by domestic channels such as
Zee TV and Sun TV into Indian homes.
However the  programmes on entertainment have driven the
market. Programmes on agriculture are minimum in the satellite channels. The
following is the programme schedule for agricultural programmes in Tamil Nadu.
